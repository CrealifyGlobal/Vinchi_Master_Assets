[[Class_Capability]]

Class Name:
Capability

Behavioral Purpose:
**Capability** serves as a **functional expression of what an organization or system is reliably able to do**. It reflects the **enduring ability to execute, perform, or deliver outcomes** at scale and with consistency—enabled by processes, resources, and technology, and aligned with strategic or operational goals.

Core Behaviors:
- **Outcome-Oriented** – Capabilities exist to produce specific, repeatable outcomes.
- **Enabling** – They serve as foundational enablers of business processes and strategic execution.
- **Composite** – Often composed of resources, knowledge, tools, and coordination across departments.
- **Evolving** – Matures over time through investment, learning, and optimization.
- **Measured** – Regularly assessed via performance indicators (KPIs) and capability maturity models.

Interaction Targets:
- **Processes** – Capabilities enable or constrain the design and execution of business workflows.
- **Strategic Objectives** – Capabilities are required to realize business ambitions.
- **Resources** – Capabilities require talent, tools, data, and infrastructure.
- **Technology** – Often dependent on or reinforced by platforms, systems, or digital enablers.
- **KPIs** – Evaluated for efficiency, effectiveness, and value contribution.

Sensing Triggers:
- **Strategy Definition** – When mapping what is required to achieve new goals.
- **Process Optimization** – When analyzing gaps in execution or service delivery.
- **Capability Mapping or Modeling Initiatives** – As part of enterprise architecture, IT strategy, or transformation.
- **Maturity Assessments** – During audits or organizational reviews.

Decision Rules:
- `capabilityMaturity = "Developing"` → Must be associated with a defined capability uplift plan.
- `capabilityType = "Technical"` → Must be linked to at least one enabling technology.
- `capabilityImpact = "High"` AND `capabilityStatus = "Active"` → Must be monitored by one or more KPIs.
- `capabilityStatus = "Retired"` → Cannot be linked to active strategic objectives or processes.

Adaptation Behavior:
- **Maturity-Responsive** – Evolves from basic to advanced as investments, practices, and resources develop.
- **Environmentally Aware** – Responds to shifts in strategy, competition, regulation, and technology.
- **Process-Aligned** – Changes in capabilities often signal or require changes in supporting processes.
- **Portfolio-Sensitive** – Capabilities interact and co-evolve; one capability’s shift may require adaptation in others.

Lifecycle Phases:
- **Identification** – Named and scoped based on strategic needs or operating model reviews.
- **Modeling** – Linked to supporting elements such as technology, processes, and outcomes.
- **Assessment** – Evaluated for performance, maturity, and alignment.
- **Uplift** – Developed or improved through investments and interventions.
- **Sunset or Transfer** – Retired when no longer required or absorbed by other capabilities.

Special Traits:
- **Transversal Anchor** – Capabilities are often reused or referenced across strategy, technology, process, and org design.
- **Granularity-Scalable** – Can be modeled at macro (enterprise) or micro (departmental/function) levels.
- **Differentiation Source** – Key differentiating capabilities are core to competitive advantage.
- **Maturity-Dependent** – The value and risk profile of a capability depends on how mature and resilient it is.

Fine-Tuning Parameters:
- **Strategic Criticality:** 9.0/10 – Determines what is feasible to achieve.
- **Change Frequency:** 6.5/10 – Capabilities evolve more slowly than projects or processes.
- **Operational Dependency:** 9.5/10 – Many processes fail or succeed based on capability effectiveness.
- **Maturity Sensitivity:** 8.7/10 – Capabilities are often central to transformation roadmaps.
