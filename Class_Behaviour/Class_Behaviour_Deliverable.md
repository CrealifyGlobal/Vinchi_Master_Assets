[[Class_Deliverable]]

Class Name:
Deliverable

Behavioral Purpose:
A **Deliverable** functions as a **concrete output** produced within the scope of a project, program, or work process. It serves as a **control point for progress**, a **requirement for validation or acceptance**, and a **binding element** between work performed and outcomes achieved.

Core Behaviors:
- **Submission Gate:** Must be created, reviewed, and often approved before triggering dependent actions (e.g., milestone achievement, billing, compliance).
- **Validation Anchor:** Requires evaluation by designated roles or authorities (e.g., QA, compliance teams, customers).
- **Progress Artifact:** Acts as a material signal of phase or task completion.
- **Compliance Instrument:** When regulatory, serves as formal proof of adherence to standards.
- **Phase Transition Driver:** In many cases, deliverables unlock transitions across project phases.

Interaction Targets:
- **Projects** or **Programs**, which define and track their production.
- **Milestones**, to which deliverables are tied as prerequisites or substantiations.
- **Compliance Standards**, when regulatory deliverables are mandated.
- **Work Systems** and **Work Processes**, which describe the operational mechanics of production.
- **Review Roles or Review Systems**, which evaluate deliverable quality and sufficiency.

Sensing Triggers:
- Completion of contributing **activities or work packages**.
- Fulfillment of **acceptance criteria** defined in SOPs or contracts.
- Receipt of all required **inputs and approvals** for submission.
- Alignment with a milestone’s planned delivery date.
- Change in **project status** (e.g., move to “Submission” stage).

Decision Rules:
- Must be associated with a **Project** or **Program**.
- Must have a **type** (e.g., Final, Phase, Regulatory) and a **status** (e.g., Submitted, Approved).
- Regulatory deliverables require reference to one or more **Compliance Standards**.
- If linked to a **Milestone**, it must be **approved** or **submitted** before the milestone is considered achieved.
- Deliverables must be **reviewed and validated** unless otherwise specified by governance policies.

Adaptation Behavior:
- **Date Drift:** Due dates may adjust based on schedule compression, delays, or scope changes.
- **Status Progression:** States evolve from Pending → Submitted → Approved or Rejected.
- **Reviewer Reassignment:** The reviewing role may shift based on organizational capacity or scope changes.
- **Versioning Behavior:** Deliverables may exist in multiple versions requiring traceability and audit logs.

Lifecycle Phases:
- **Defined:** Deliverable is specified in scope or contract.
- **In Progress:** Under creation or assembly.
- **Submitted:** Delivered for review.
- **Approved or Rejected:** Final disposition.
- **Archived:** Closed and stored as evidence or institutional record.

Special Traits:
- **Output-Oriented:** Unlike Milestones, Deliverables are results of action.
- **Externally Visible:** Can be accessed by customers, regulators, or stakeholders.
- **Evaluative:** Requires validation or rejection based on pre-established criteria.
- **Policy-Governed:** Often constrained by standard operating procedures or external regulations.

Fine-Tuning Parameters:
- **Persistence Score:** 9/10 — Deliverables often persist as institutional records, evidence, or reusable assets.
- **Flexibility Score:** 5/10 — Dates and contents may shift during execution, but required status is fixed.
- **Influence Radius:** 8/10 — Impacts milestones, compliance, billing, reviews, and phase transitions.
- **Sensitivity to Change Triggers:** 7/10 — Shifts in scope, schedule, or stakeholder input can affect production and approval.
