[[Class_KnowledgeArtifact]]

Class Name:
Knowledge Artifact

Behavioral Purpose:
A **Knowledge Artifact** acts as a **carrier and multiplier of organizational memory, operational clarity, and strategic insight**. Its purpose is to **embed, preserve, and propagate knowledge across people, processes, and systems**—enabling AI, humans, and automation agents to act with contextual intelligence and evidence-informed rationale.

Core Behaviors:
- **Codify & Externalize Knowledge** – Translates experience, insight, data, and decisions into persistent, retrievable form.
- **Support Operational Execution** – Provides reference for workflows, SOPs, and task-specific know-how.
- **Enable Strategic Reasoning** – Grounds business choices and governance in historical, regulatory, or analytical logic.
- **Fuel Machine Reasoning** – Serves as structured input to LLMs, search engines, and knowledge graphs (RAG/RAG-Fusion).

Interaction Targets:
- **Business Processes** – Artifacts are used to teach, trigger, or validate operational steps.
- **Decisions & Strategy** – Evidence for justification, traceability, or simulation.
- **Compliance Frameworks** – Documentation trail to prove adherence to laws and policies.
- **Learning Programs** – Source content for onboarding, training, and capability uplift.
- **Digital Agents & Systems** – Used as training, context, or response sources in AI workflows.

Sensing Triggers:
- **Tacit-to-Explicit Need** – When expert knowledge is at risk of loss, artifacts are created for preservation.
- **Regulatory/Compliance Action** – Documentation of policy, procedure, or certification evidence.
- **Decision Memory Need** – To reconstruct logic behind decisions or audit strategy assumptions.
- **Operational Knowledge Gaps** – Identified gaps in execution or repeat errors prompt artifact creation.

Decision Rules:
- A **Knowledge Artifact** must be traceable to at least one Business Process, Decision, or Learning Program.
- `artifactType = Tacit Knowledge Capture` requires human-source origin and formalization pathway.
- `artifactAccessibility = Public` must pass a governance and compliance review.
- Artifacts with `artifactRetentionPeriod = Long-Term or Permanent` must be indexed and version-controlled.

Adaptation Behavior:
- **Version Evolution** – Artifacts are periodically reviewed, refined, or deprecated based on relevance.
- **Format Diversification** – A single knowledge unit may take multiple forms (PDF + Audio + Ontology).
- **Usage Feedback Loops** – Access frequency, relevance scoring, or AI effectiveness can signal need for updates.
- **Semantic Linking** – Artifacts become more powerful when linked across ontologies, roles, and events.

Lifecycle Phases:
- **Draft** – Created, unvalidated knowledge unit in review.
- **Published** – Approved and deployed in knowledge ecosystem.
- **Revised** – Modified based on new information, feedback, or context.
- **Deprecated** – Archived or superseded but still retained for reference.
- **Converted** – Transformed into a different format (e.g., manual to chatbot training data).

Special Traits:
- **Indexable & Referenceable** – Each artifact has a unique identity and retrievable address in knowledge space.
- **Polyformatic** – Can exist as text, video, image, diagram, dataset, transcript, or model.
- **Interoperable** – Usable by both humans and machines (e.g., AI models, RAG pipelines, LMS systems).
- **Retention-Linked** – Often tied to legal, operational, or contractual lifespans.

Fine-Tuning Parameters:
- **AI-Usability Score:** 8.5/10 – Highly useful in RAG and semantic search with structure and tagging.
- **Knowledge Density:** 9/10 – Represents condensed, reusable intellectual capital.
- **Context Sensitivity:** 7/10 – Usage and relevance vary significantly by domain and timing.
- **Auditability:** 9/10 – Traceable origin, authorship, use case, and lifespan.
