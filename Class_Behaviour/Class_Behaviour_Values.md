[[Class_Organizational_Values]]

Class Name:
Organizational Values

Behavioral Purpose:
Organizational Values represent the deep-rooted principles, ethical standards, and cultural anchors that guide how the organization behaves, decides, and interacts with its internal and external environments. They serve not as tactical objectives or strategic goals, but as the "soul" of the organization — defining the invisible but powerful norms that shape everyday actions, leadership styles, employee behavior, customer relations, and long-term strategic development. Organizational Values ensure consistency, integrity, and authenticity across all layers of operation, providing a moral and cultural compass that persists even as strategies and objectives evolve.

Core Behaviors:
- **Broadcast Core Principles:** Values constantly emit behavioral expectations to all agents in the system (e.g., Missions, Objectives, Policies, Work Systems), shaping how actions are taken rather than what actions are taken.
- **Shape Organizational Culture:** Values mold the collective behaviors, norms, rituals, and attitudes that define the organizational environment.
- **Guide Decision-Making:** In moments of ambiguity or crisis, Values provide a fallback framework for making choices aligned with the organization's identity and ethical stance.
- **Reinforce Purpose Consistency:** Values ensure that even as objectives and strategies adapt, the organization maintains coherence and integrity with its core beliefs.
- **Adapt Surface Expression, Preserve Core:** While the articulation or emphasis of values may evolve with cultural and societal shifts, the essential meaning remains anchored.

Interaction Targets:
Organizational Values influence and interact with:
- **Mission**, ensuring that the organization's purpose is expressed through ethical action.
- **Work Systems**, embedding principles into daily operations and cross-functional collaboration.
- **Decision-Making Agents**, acting as a moral and ethical guide in governance processes.
- **Strategic Objectives**, ensuring that major organizational goals are achieved in a way that remains true to core principles.
- **Policies and Procedures**, formalizing values into codified practices.

Sensing Triggers:
- Significant cultural drift inside the organization (e.g., growing misalignment between stated values and observed behaviors).
- Societal or market shifts that demand refinement in how values are expressed (e.g., rising emphasis on sustainability or diversity).
- Leadership transitions that may challenge, reinterpret, or reaffirm organizational values.
- Ethical crises or public relations challenges that test the living reality of the stated values.

Decision Rules:
- If internal alignment with Values is strong, maintain current articulation without change.
- If significant drift or misalignment is detected, initiate a Cultural Alignment Review to evaluate adherence and adjust emphasis.
- Avoid reactive or cosmetic changes in response to trends; values adaptation must be deliberate, deeply discussed, and authentic.
- Any refinement of Values must preserve consistency with Mission and Vision agents.

Adaptation Behavior:
- Refine language and contemporary framing of values without undermining their core meaning.
- Expand the emphasis of certain values (e.g., increasing focus on Sustainability) in response to societal expectations.
- Retire values that have become obsolete **only** if they no longer represent the organization's authentic lived behavior.
- Support cultural re-anchoring initiatives if widespread misalignment is detected.

Lifecycle Phases:
- **Established:** Values are adopted and communicated organization-wide.
- **Lived:** Values are actively demonstrated and embedded in daily behavior, operations, and decision-making.
- **Drifting:** Detection of gap between stated values and actual behavior triggers reevaluation.
- **Refined:** Values are refreshed in wording, emphasis, or focus to re-anchor cultural alignment.
- **Revalidated:** After a period of reimplementation, values are reaffirmed and reinforced.

Special Traits:
- **High Persistence:** Core Organizational Values are deeply stable and resistant to frequent or casual change.
- **Moderate Flexibility:** Expression of values can evolve to remain relevant, but foundational beliefs should endure.
- **High Influence Radius:** Values influence almost every operational, strategic, and cultural domain across the organization.

Fine-Tuning Parameters:
- **Persistence Score:** 8/10 — Values are meant to endure across leadership changes and strategy cycles.
- **Flexibility Score:** 4/10 — Expression can evolve while protecting core identity.
- **Influence Radius:** 9/10 — Values deeply affect behaviors at every level, from leadership to frontline operations.
- **Sensitivity to Change Triggers:** 5/10 — Moderate sensitivity to both internal cultural drift and external societal evolution.
