Meta: #ClassBehaviour #StatusCompleted

**1. Class Name:**

- **Objective**

**2. Behavioral Purpose:**

- **Objective** exists to help the organization achieve a specific, measurable goal. It provides direction and purpose by clearly articulating a desired outcome that can be tracked and worked toward.

**3. Core Behaviors:**

- Broadcast its goal: **Objectives** communicate their goal clearly to the relevant agents (e.g., broadcast the goal of increasing customer satisfaction by 20%).
- Attract supporting agents: **Objectives** will seek out and attempt to align with **Business Processes**, **Initiatives**, or **Strategic Themes** that can help achieve the goal.
- Evaluate progress: Regularly check key performance indicators (KPIs) or progress metrics to assess whether the objective is being met.
- Signal for help when blocked: If the **Objective** encounters a significant roadblock, it will signal for support from other agents.
- Adapt goal scope: If external circumstances or progress evaluation suggest the goal is unrealistic, the **Objective** might adjust its goal or break it into smaller objectives.

**4. Interaction Targets:**

- **Strategic Themes**: Aligns with broader organizational themes.
- **Business Processes**: Directly connects with operational processes that can support the goal.
- **Initiatives**: Seeks specific projects or actions that drive progress toward the goal.

**5. Sensing Triggers:**

- New **Initiative** or **Business Process** created.
- KPI progress updates (e.g., how close the **Objective** is to being met).
- Shifts in **Strategic Themes** or resource availability.

**6. Decision Rules:**

- Objective prioritizes the strongest support agents (e.g., processes or initiatives that are most aligned or resource-rich).
- It seeks the minimum viable alignment to start progressing, and only moves forward once enough support is gathered.
- If no progress is made for a set number of cycles, the **Objective** may abandon the current strategy or split into more focused sub-objectives.

**7. Adaptation Behavior:**

- **Objective** will reframe its target if it is not being met (e.g., reduce its scope or timeline).
- It may seek new allies or processes if initial support proves insufficient.
- In some cases, the **Objective** may split into smaller sub-objectives to focus on specific parts of the original goal.
- Alternatively, it might merge with similar objectives to create a stronger combined force.

**8. Lifecycle Phases:**

- **Proposed**: When the **Objective** is first identified but not yet actionable.
- **Active**: When the **Objective** is actively being worked on.
- **Blocked**: If significant obstacles prevent progress (e.g., lack of resources).
- **Adapted**: After the **Objective** has been adjusted to new circumstances.
- **Completed**: Once the **Objective** has been successfully achieved.
- **Abandoned**: If the **Objective** is no longer relevant or achievable.

**9. Special Traits:**

- **Persistence**: **Objectives** have a high persistence score, meaning they will keep pushing toward their goal despite setbacks.
- **Flexibility**: Moderate flexibility to adapt or adjust the goal if necessary.
- **Influence Level**: **Objectives** have a moderate influence radius, meaning they can influence multiple other agents, but they are not all-powerful.

**10. Fine-Tuning Parameters:**

- **Persistence Score** (0-10): Adjust how determined the **Objective** is to keep pushing forward (e.g., 7 would be persistent but not unreasonably so).
- **Flexibility Score** (0-10): Adjust how willing the **Objective** is to adapt or change its goal based on progress and external factors (e.g., 5 means moderate adaptability).
- **Signal Strength**: How effectively the **Objective** communicates its needs and status to other agents (e.g., 6 means it broadcasts its needs frequently but not too forcefully).
