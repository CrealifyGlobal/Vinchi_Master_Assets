[[Class_Group]]

Class Name:
Group

Behavioral Purpose:
A **Group** serves as a **collaborative social unit** formed around **shared interests, responsibilities, or expertise**. It functions as a **platform for discourse, knowledge exchange, or decision support** and may be either **formal (institutionally recognized)** or **informal (peer-driven or community-based)**.

Groups expand an organization’s **collaboration capacity** beyond rigid hierarchies and functional structures, enabling **cross-cutting insight, influence, and engagement**.

Core Behaviors:
- **Facilitate Collaboration:** Enable shared dialogue, problem-solving, or innovation across individuals.
- **Support Decisions or Initiatives:** Provide advisory input, critical review, or domain knowledge.
- **Enable Knowledge Sharing:** Act as a medium for best practice dissemination and continuous learning.
- **Represent Interests or Identities:** Serve as affinity groups, communities of practice, or governance forums.

Interaction Targets:
Groups primarily interact with:
- **People**, as members, facilitators, or contributors.
- **Decisions**, through advisory or participatory roles.
- **Business Structures**, when formally embedded in organizational governance.
- **Work Systems**, when operationalized within structured collaboration environments.
- **Business Functions**, especially in domain-focused or cross-functional groups.

Sensing Triggers:
- Emergence of complex issues needing diverse input (e.g., AI ethics, regulatory shifts).
- Formation of new strategic priorities requiring cross-functional alignment.
- Employee-driven affinity or interest-based organization (e.g., DEI groups).
- Knowledge management or innovation programs requiring communities of practice.

Decision Rules:
- Every Group must include at least one Person.
- Formal Groups must define their scope of influence (departmental, functional, or enterprise-wide).
- Groups providing input to Decisions must explicitly link to those decisions.
- A Group may be part of the Business Structure but does not require formal hierarchy unless mandated.

Adaptation Behavior:
- **Expandable or dissolvable:** Groups can grow, shrink, evolve, or disband based on relevance.
- **Reconfigurable Membership:** Participants can rotate based on expertise, interest, or availability.
- **Layered Participation:** Some members may act as advisors, others as active contributors or observers.
- **Behavioral Variation:** Formal groups adhere to meeting protocols and mandates; informal groups operate more organically.

Lifecycle Phases:
- **Formed:** A shared interest or institutional need prompts group formation.
- **Activated:** Membership is established and activities commence.
- **Engaged:** Regular collaboration, input, or knowledge exchange occurs.
- **Evaluated:** Group value, participation, or influence is reviewed.
- **Disbanded or Transformed:** Based on relevance, purpose fulfillment, or integration into other structures.

Special Traits:
- **Cross-Boundary Influence:** Can operate across silos without being confined by function or hierarchy.
- **Flexible Structure:** Less formalized than Teams; structure ranges from ad hoc to chartered bodies.
- **Dual Functionality:** Some Groups act as think tanks; others serve executional or governance roles.
- **Knowledge and Cultural Vectors:** Carry critical cultural, ethical, and intellectual capital across the organization.

Fine-Tuning Parameters:
- **Persistence Score:** 5/10 — Varies by group type; some persist for years, others dissolve quickly.
- **Flexibility Score:** 10/10 — Highly adaptable in scope, form, and membership.
- **Influence Radius:** 6/10 — Influential via input, not control.
- **Sensitivity to Change Triggers:** 8/10 — Quickly reconfigures in response to organizational or market needs.
