[[Class_Opportunity]]

Class Name:
Opportunity

Behavioral Purpose:
An **Opportunity** acts as a **potential value lever**—a signal of **future possibility** that, when acted upon, can **generate growth, innovation, or strategic advantage**. It guides forward-looking decision-making and resource deployment by **highlighting favorable market, technology, or operational conditions**.

Core Behaviors:
- **Emerging** – Identified through trend recognition, innovation, or strategic gap analysis.
- **Evaluated** – Assessed for alignment with strategic objectives, potential return, and feasibility.
- **Framed** – Positioned in terms of value creation, competitive edge, or risk-adjusted return.
- **Activated** – Pursued via roadmaps, projects, or investment plans.
- **Monitored** – Tracked over time to determine relevance, conversion, or expiration.

Interaction Targets:
- **Market** – Opportunity space defined by customer needs, gaps, or trends.
- **Strategic Objective** – Justifies pursuit by aligning with long-term business direction.
- **Technology** – Often rooted in new capabilities that enable novel value delivery.
- **Financial Resource** – Opportunities depend on capital allocation to be realized.
- **Compliance** – Regulatory contexts may constrain or shape opportunity pursuit.

Sensing Triggers:
- **Environmental Scanning** – Detected through PESTLE, SWOT, trend mapping, or foresight tools.
- **Customer Signals** – Emerging demand, unmet needs, or shifting behaviors.
- **Innovation Pipeline** – Technological advancements enabling new value propositions.
- **Competitive Gap** – Identified whitespace in positioning, offerings, or capabilities.
- **Operational Insights** – Internal performance data or inefficiencies indicating leverage points.

Decision Rules:
- `opportunityType = "Technology Opportunity"` → Must link to at least one enabling Technology.
- `opportunityStatus = "Pursuing"` → Requires associated Execution Plan or Roadmap.
- `opportunityType = "Investment Opportunity"` → Must be backed by a defined Financial Resource.
- `opportunityPotential >= 20%` → Prioritize for feasibility assessment or business case development.
- `opportunityTimeframe = "Short-Term"` → Flag for near-term execution readiness.

Adaptation Behavior:
- **Responsive Acceleration** – When market shifts favor the opportunity, fast-tracked into execution.
- **Reprioritization** – Opportunities are re-ranked as context or potential evolves.
- **Opportunity Clustering** – Multiple related opportunities may be bundled into strategic themes.
- **Conversion to Initiative** – Promising opportunities transform into formal projects or programs.

Lifecycle Phases:
- **Detection** – Opportunity signal appears through research, market analysis, or internal ideation.
- **Evaluation** – Potential, feasibility, and strategic alignment are assessed.
- **Framing** – Opportunity is defined, scoped, and communicated (e.g., in presentations or cases).
- **Decision** – Chosen for pursuit, deferred, or dismissed based on prioritization.
- **Activation** – Converted into actionable plans, budgets, or cross-functional projects.

Special Traits:
- **Latent Value** – Represents potential, not yet realized impact.
- **Cross-Cutting** – Can span multiple domains: market, product, strategy, technology.
- **Time-Sensitive** – Windows of opportunity may close; urgency is often context-dependent.
- **Uncertain Return** – Involves assumptions, forecasting, and probabilistic outcomes.

Fine-Tuning Parameters:
- **Speculative Volatility:** 8.2/10 – Uncertain realization but potentially high reward.
- **Strategic Leverage Potential:** 9.1/10 – Strong alignment with growth or transformation.
- **Time Sensitivity Index:** 7.4/10 – Many opportunities have expiry or timing risk.
- **Cross-Domain Influence:** 8.5/10 – Often affects multiple strategic or operational layers.
