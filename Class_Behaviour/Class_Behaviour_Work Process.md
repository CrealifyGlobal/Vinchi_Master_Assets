[[Class_Work_Process]]

Class Name:
Work Process

Behavioral Purpose:
Work Processes define the structured, operational-level sequences of tasks that carry out the work within a Work System. They focus on how specific activities are executed, coordinated, and measured—often at the level of individual roles, small teams, or cross-role handoffs. Work Processes ensure daily operations are efficient, consistent, and aligned with business goals. While Business Processes operate at a higher level of functional orchestration, Work Processes are the practical building blocks of execution—bringing policies, tools, and task design together into repeatable workflows.

Core Behaviors:
- **Translate Work System Design into Action:** Work Processes are how strategy becomes execution at the task level, turning structures into outcomes.
- **Deliver Operational Efficiency:** By being measured, refined, and standardized, Work Processes drive performance improvements and quality control.
- **Bridge Human and Automated Workflows:** Work Processes blend manual steps with automated systems, especially in hybrid or digitized environments.
- **Support Accountability and Measurement:** Work Processes define clear steps and responsibilities, enabling tracking of time, quality, and impact.

Interaction Targets:
Work Processes primarily interact with:
- **Work Systems**, within which they operate and to which they contribute structure and rhythm.
- **Tasks**, which form the individual steps of a Work Process.
- **Policies**, ensuring the process aligns with compliance, governance, and quality requirements.
- **Resources**, such as staff, time, data, and tools needed to execute tasks.
- **Metrics**, which capture key performance indicators and support process optimization.

Sensing Triggers:
- Performance metrics indicate low efficiency, high error rates, or poor throughput.
- Organizational or technological changes impact task design or resource availability.
- Policy or compliance shifts require updated execution standards.
- Customer feedback or audit findings suggest breakdowns in execution consistency.

Decision Rules:
- Each Work Process must be part of a Work System and must consist of defined Tasks.
- Select the appropriate Process Type (e.g., manual, automated, hybrid) based on business need, risk, and scalability.
- Routinely review efficiency and performance indicators to guide process improvement efforts.
- Align process execution with defined KPIs and compliance requirements.

Adaptation Behavior:
- Shift from manual to automated or hybrid models as technology adoption increases.
- Redesign task sequences and responsibilities when bottlenecks or duplication are detected.
- Introduce AI-driven or event-triggered processes to enhance responsiveness and agility.
- Integrate continuous improvement practices (e.g., Kaizen, Lean Six Sigma) for ongoing refinement.

Lifecycle Phases:
- **Defined:** The process is documented, sequenced, and assigned to a responsible team or role.
- **Enabled:** Required tools, technologies, and training are in place to support execution.
- **Executed:** Tasks are performed routinely or on demand, within defined parameters.
- **Monitored:** KPIs and performance data are tracked to ensure process health.
- **Optimized:** Based on performance insights or evolving needs, the process is refined or redesigned.

Special Traits:
- **Execution-Focused:** Work Processes are the true “doing” engine of the organization.
- **Measurable and Improvable:** Every process can be quantified and optimized.
- **Context-Aware:** While structured, many Work Processes can adapt in real-time to inputs, exceptions, and human judgment.
- **Technology-Enabled:** Modern Work Processes are increasingly augmented or automated by digital systems.

Fine-Tuning Parameters:
- **Persistence Score:** 8/10 — Processes often endure over time, though they evolve with tools and strategies.
- **Flexibility Score:** 7/10 — Adaptive Work Processes allow variation; routine processes favor consistency.
- **Influence Radius:** 8/10 — Work Processes drive performance, quality, and employee experience at the operational core.
- **Sensitivity to Change Triggers:** 8/10 — Highly responsive to system changes, policy updates, and efficiency pressures.
