[[Class_Management_Process]]

Class Name:
Management Process

Behavioral Purpose:
Management Processes are the strategic and operational scaffolding through which organizations oversee, coordinate, and continuously improve their work. They focus on planning, control, performance monitoring, and policy enforcement rather than direct execution. While Work Processes and Business Processes focus on “doing,” Management Processes focus on “ensuring things are done right”—that goals are aligned, risks are managed, rules are followed, and improvements are made. These processes shape leadership behavior, define governance structures, and sustain the health and accountability of the organization.

Core Behaviors:
- **Guide Organizational Direction:** Management Processes define and adjust strategic objectives, priorities, and resource alignment.
- **Monitor Execution Quality:** Through oversight of Business and Work Processes, they ensure compliance, quality, and efficiency.
- **Enforce Policy and Governance:** They implement internal controls and ensure regulatory alignment through systematic audits and checks.
- **Enable Continuous Improvement:** Feedback loops, performance reviews, and structured decision cycles lead to learning and adaptive change.

Interaction Targets:
Management Processes primarily interact with:
- **Strategic Objectives**, translating vision into measurable execution mechanisms.
- **Policies**, applying regulatory or internal control frameworks.
- **Business Processes**, which they oversee for optimization and alignment.
- **Work Processes**, which they directly govern or influence.
- **Metrics**, used to measure and track the success and maturity of managerial oversight.

Sensing Triggers:
- Performance deviations (e.g., KPI shortfalls, rising error rates) indicating the need for intervention or adjustment.
- Changes in the regulatory environment requiring updates to oversight or compliance routines.
- Strategic redirection necessitating new planning, governance, or resource allocation models.
- Internal signals (e.g., audit findings, stakeholder feedback) highlighting inefficiencies or control gaps.

Decision Rules:
- All Management Processes must be connected to at least one Strategic Objective and linked to monitored Business or governed Work Processes.
- Governance-driven processes must reference a formal Policy framework and define key performance metrics.
- The management level (strategic, tactical, or operational) determines the scope of authority and review cycle.
- Compliance score and review frequency must be periodically assessed and adjusted.

Adaptation Behavior:
- Expand in scope and complexity as organizations grow or face increased external scrutiny.
- Transition from informal practices to formal processes during maturity shifts or certification requirements.
- Integrate automation or AI for decision support in performance management and risk analysis.
- Recalibrate control thresholds and decision rules in response to operational feedback or regulatory audits.

Lifecycle Phases:
- **Designed:** Management scope, rules, and responsibilities are formally defined.
- **Activated:** Roles, schedules, and supporting systems (dashboards, policies, documentation) are deployed.
- **Executed:** Management routines (e.g., reviews, approvals, escalations) are performed regularly.
- **Measured:** KPIs and control reports are used to assess performance and compliance.
- **Refined:** Based on outcomes and insights, the process is improved or redesigned.

Special Traits:
- **Control-Oriented:** Focused on performance integrity, governance, and risk mitigation.
- **Cross-Hierarchical:** Functions across strategic, tactical, and operational levels.
- **Standardization-Driven:** Often tied to industry or regulatory standards such as ISO, ITIL, or COBIT.
- **Feedback-Rich:** Designed to learn from what is happening in the system and drive improvement.

Fine-Tuning Parameters:
- **Persistence Score:** 9/10 — Management Processes are enduring structures in stable organizations.
- **Flexibility Score:** 6/10 — Flexibility varies by process type (e.g., audits are rigid, strategic planning can be fluid).
- **Influence Radius:** 9/10 — These processes shape nearly all layers of performance, from compliance to innovation.
- **Sensitivity to Change Triggers:** 8/10 — Highly reactive to risk, compliance, and strategic shift signals.
