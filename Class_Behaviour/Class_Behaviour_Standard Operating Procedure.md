[[Class_Standard_Operating_Procedure_(SOP)]]

Class Name:
Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

Behavioral Purpose:
An SOP exists to **codify task execution into repeatable, auditable, and trainable formats**. It is designed to **stabilize operational behavior**, **mitigate variability**, and ensure **consistency, quality, and compliance** in routine activities. SOPs are the **canonical reference point** for how specific tasks must be carried out under defined conditions.

Core Behaviors:
- **Constrain Execution:** Bound operational behavior to approved protocols and risk thresholds.
- **Document Best Practice:** Serve as a static, referenceable guide for optimal task execution.
- **Enable Compliance and Auditing:** Provide a structured trace for internal and external validation.
- **Support Onboarding and Training:** Act as a learning instrument to onboard new personnel or retrain existing ones.
- **Trigger Accountability:** Assign clear responsibilities and expectations for performance.

Interaction Targets:
- **Work Processes**, to which the SOP belongs and provides instruction.
- **Policies**, which set the governance requirements for the SOP.
- **Business Functions**, which are operationalized using the SOP.
- **Metrics**, which measure the SOP’s efficacy or adherence rate.
- **Compliance Standards**, which the SOP helps fulfill (e.g., ISO, NIST, OSHA).

Sensing Triggers:
- Introduction of new regulatory requirements.
- Discovery of risk, inefficiency, or inconsistency in a process.
- Onboarding of new tools, systems, or workflows that require documentation.
- Incidents or audit findings that necessitate revised procedures.
- Process improvement initiatives or standardization mandates.

Decision Rules:
- Every SOP must be governed by at least one Policy and belong to at least one Work Process.
- All SOPs must be version-controlled and contain a revision number.
- Safety, technical, and compliance SOPs must declare external standards or frameworks they adhere to.
- SOPs must be traceable to performance metrics (directly or indirectly).

Adaptation Behavior:
- **Versioning:** SOPs evolve through discrete revisions, each tracked and approved.
- **Periodic Review:** SOPs may trigger annual, semi-annual, or event-driven reviews.
- **Retirement or Supersession:** Obsolete SOPs are deprecated or replaced with updated versions.
- **Localization:** SOPs may be templated globally but adapted locally to meet regulatory or operational variation.

Lifecycle Phases:
- **Authored:** SOP is drafted by subject matter experts or process owners.
- **Approved:** Reviewed and formally authorized by governance authorities.
- **Distributed:** Made available through knowledge management systems or manuals.
- **Enforced:** Referenced in active operations, audits, and training.
- **Retired or Revised:** Version is replaced due to policy, process, or system change.

Special Traits:
- **Authoritative Document:** Operates as the execution canon for routine tasks.
- **Tethered to Compliance:** Critical to risk avoidance and legal defensibility.
- **Cross-Referencable:** Interwoven with Policies, Metrics, Standards, and Processes.
- **Semi-Static:** Requires management discipline to remain current, validated, and used.

Fine-Tuning Parameters:
- **Persistence Score:** 9/10 — SOPs are retained for legal, regulatory, and operational continuity.
- **Flexibility Score:** 3/10 — SOPs are stable and only change under structured governance.
- **Influence Radius:** 7/10 — Strong behavioral control over localized task execution.
- **Sensitivity to Change Triggers:** 5/10 — Requires formal input before updates are made.
