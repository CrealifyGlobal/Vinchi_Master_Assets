[[Class_MindModel]]

Class Name:
Mind Model

Behavioral Purpose:
A **Mind Model** encodes a **cognitive reasoning pattern** that guides **how an agent—human or machine—perceives, analyzes, and acts**. It serves as a mental scaffold for **decision-making, problem-solving, and pattern recognition** in complex or uncertain environments.

Core Behaviors:
- **Structure Thought** – Provides a repeatable logic pattern for making sense of information.
- **Guide Decisions** – Influences how choices are framed, weighted, and prioritized.
- **Enable Transfer** – Offers a transferable mental structure across contexts or domains.
- **Activate Abstraction** – Facilitates high-level conceptualization and simplification of complexity.
- **Shape Learning** – Trains AI and humans alike on how to organize knowledge for strategic impact.

Interaction Targets:
- **Decisions** – Serves as a reasoning substrate for informed judgment.
- **Strategic Plans** – Embeds thinking models into future-oriented initiatives.
- **Risk Assessments** – Frames how uncertainty and variance are evaluated.
- **Knowledge Artifacts** – Translates into reusable structures for training, AI modeling, or simulations.
- **Artificial Intelligence** – Functions as an interpretive logic layer for cognitive systems.

Sensing Triggers:
- **Ambiguity or Complexity** – Engages when clear answers are lacking or data is incomplete.
- **Need for Structured Thinking** – Triggered when unstructured problems require a framing lens.
- **AI Reasoning Request** – Activated during inference cycles to model thought paths.
- **Scenario Exploration** – Used in foresight, simulation, or counterfactual analysis.

Decision Rules:
- `modelType = Strategic OR Decision-Making` should be prioritized in **planning cycles**.
- `modelComplexityLevel > 0.8` implies **expert knowledge required** for operationalization.
- `modelValidationStatus = Deprecated` triggers model removal or migration workflow.
- If a Mind Model is applied in **AI**, it must be compatible with **semantic reasoning** and **symbolic logic structures**.

Adaptation Behavior:
- **Pattern Transposition** – Reframed across domains while maintaining core logical structure.
- **Heuristic Derivation** – Extracted into rules, prompts, or simulation engines.
- **Abstraction** – Generalized into strategic archetypes or guiding philosophies.
- **Parameterization** – Converted into inputs for AI agents or decision tools.

Lifecycle Phases:
- **Proposed** – Defined in theory or sourced from expert logic.
- **Validated** – Proven useful through simulation, modeling, or field deployment.
- **Embedded** – Operationalized into tooling, systems, or governance frameworks.
- **Deprecated** – Replaced when surpassed by more accurate, adaptive, or explainable models.

Special Traits:
- **Cognitively Composable** – Can be **nested, layered, or combined** to form meta-mental models.
- **Human-AI Symbiotic** – Easily interpreted by both human strategists and symbolic reasoning agents.
- **Cross-Contextual** – Applicable across industries, disciplines, and operating environments.
- **Introspectable** – Encourages reflection on how thinking is structured and bias is introduced.

Fine-Tuning Parameters:
- **AI-Usability Score:** 9.2/10 – Ideal for interpretive, symbolic, and hybrid AI systems.
- **Knowledge Generalization Score:** 8.7/10 – Translates across sectors with minimal transformation.
- **Logic Encoding Strength:** 9.5/10 – Naturally maps to semantic graphs, if-then structures, and probabilistic networks.
- **Cognitive Transparency:** 8.9/10 – Enhances explainability and user trust in decisions.
