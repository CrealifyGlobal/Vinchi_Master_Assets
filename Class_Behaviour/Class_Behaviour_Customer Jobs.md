[[Class_CustomerJobs]]

Class Name:
Customer Jobs

Behavioral Purpose:
The **Customer Jobs** class models the **motivations, goals, and unmet needs** that drive customer behavior. It enables systems to **prioritize, map, and optimize offerings** based on what customers are truly trying to accomplish—**functionally, emotionally, or socially**—enhancing product-market fit and innovation.

Core Behaviors:
- **Need Capture & Classification:** Categorizes customer motivations as functional, emotional, social, financial, regulatory, or convenience-related.
- **Alignment Matching:** Links jobs with appropriate offerings and value propositions.
- **Priority Evaluation:** Assesses the importance and pain intensity of jobs for prioritization and roadmap influence.
- **Expectation Signaling:** Detects jobs indirectly from behavioral patterns, usage data, and feedback loops.
- **Lifecycle Mapping:** Tracks how jobs change across the customer journey (e.g., awareness vs. usage vs. retention).

Interaction Targets:
- **Customer Segment** – Defines who is performing the job.
- **Value Proposition** – Determines which solution responds to the job.
- **Offering** – Connects jobs to tangible or intangible value mechanisms.
- **Pain** – Flags blockers or frustrations tied to incomplete jobs.
- **Gain** – Illuminates the ideal outcomes customers seek to achieve.

Sensing Triggers:
- User behavior analysis (e.g., tool abandonment, help-seeking).
- Voice of the customer inputs (e.g., interviews, reviews, tickets).
- Shifts in market trends or technological capability.
- Segmentation shifts (new personas with unserved jobs).

Decision Rules:
- A Customer Job **must be linked** to at least one Offering and Customer Segment.
- Jobs with **high importance and pain levels** should receive priority in product/solution alignment.
- Jobs may belong to **multiple types** (e.g., a task can be both emotional and functional).
- A single Job may be served by **multiple Value Propositions** across different offerings.
- Jobs must be **traceable to insights from the customer experience** or demand intelligence layer.

Adaptation Behavior:
- **Dynamic Reprioritization:** As customer segments evolve or new pain points arise, jobs may be reweighted in importance.
- **Emergent Discovery:** Customer Jobs may emerge from longitudinal trend analysis and contextual feedback signals.
- **Cross-Market Application:** Identical jobs may appear in different verticals or geographies, requiring localization rather than redefinition.
- **Design Feedback Loop:** Jobs inform offering development; usage of offerings refines understanding of jobs.

Lifecycle Phases:
- **Identification:** Job is surfaced through research, feedback, or inference.
- **Definition:** Job is categorized by type and rated by importance/pain.
- **Mapping:** Job is linked to a Value Proposition, Offering, and Segment.
- **Activation:** Solutions are deployed or designed to resolve the job.
- **Reevaluation:** Feedback and outcomes are measured to refine job scope or solution fit.

Special Traits:
- **Intention-Centric:** Jobs are agnostic of product—they reflect human or business intent.
- **Emotionally Rich:** Many jobs include intangible motivations (status, control, identity).
- **Multidimensional Mapping:** Jobs may affect multiple offerings, customer types, and business capabilities simultaneously.
- **UX + Strategy Bridging:** Serves as the connective tissue between customer experience and strategic design.

Fine-Tuning Parameters:
- **Persistence Score:** 7/10 – Jobs evolve with customer maturity and market shifts but remain generally consistent within a segment.
- **Influence Score:** 9/10 – Directly drives demand, product fit, and satisfaction.
- **Sensitivity to Behavior:** 8/10 – Strong correlation between behavioral signals and job fulfillment or struggle.
- **Cross-Domain Reusability:** 9/10 – Functional and emotional jobs often replicate across domains.
