[[Class_Project]]

Class Name:
Project

Behavioral Purpose:
A **Project** exists as a **temporary vehicle for focused value creation**, enabling organizations to realize specific outcomes within controlled boundaries of time, scope, and budget. It delivers **tangible results** aligned with strategic, operational, or compliance objectives through a defined set of activities and milestones.

Core Behaviors:
- **Outcome-Oriented Execution:** Delivers a predefined product, service, or result.
- **Time-Bound Lifecycle:** Operates within a clearly defined start and end date.
- **Performance-Constrained:** Executes within scoped parameters of cost, quality, risk, and schedule.
- **Governed Delivery:** Adheres to project management frameworks and policies.
- **Alignment-Driven:** Contributes to higher-order goals within Programs or Portfolios.

Interaction Targets:
- **Programs**, which coordinate thematically related projects.
- **Project Portfolios**, which prioritize and resource project investments.
- **Strategic Objectives**, which the project ultimately supports.
- **Policies**, such as frameworks for governance, compliance, or funding.
- **Metrics**, which track progress, success, and deviation from plans.

Sensing Triggers:
- Identification of a **strategic initiative** requiring tangible deliverables.
- Detection of **gaps or opportunities** not addressable by existing operations.
- **Mandates or regulatory changes** necessitating new development or compliance.
- Lifecycle transitions in **Programs** prompting initiation or closure of projects.
- Budget cycles opening funding windows for **initiative execution**.

Decision Rules:
- Every Project must belong to at least one Program or Project Portfolio.
- A Project must have a Start Date and End Date to define its lifecycle.
- Compliance-related Projects must reference appropriate Regulatory Policies.
- All Projects must be evaluated using KPIs related to budget, time, quality, and risk.
- Projects should only be initiated if their objectives and outcomes are well-defined.

Adaptation Behavior:
- **Scope Adjustments:** Shifts in goals, deliverables, or priorities require change control.
- **Time or Budget Reforecasting:** Rebalances based on delays, acceleration, or resourcing issues.
- **Risk Response:** Changes in internal or external environments trigger mitigation strategies.
- **Escalation to Program/Portfolio:** Misalignment or systemic issues trigger upstream decision-making.

Lifecycle Phases:
- **Initiated:** Project charter, justification, and alignment are approved.
- **Planned:** Detailed scope, schedule, resource, and risk planning occurs.
- **Executed:** Tasks and deliverables are completed and monitored.
- **Monitored & Controlled:** Progress is evaluated, and deviations are managed.
- **Closed:** Deliverables are accepted, stakeholders are satisfied, and lessons learned are documented.

Special Traits:
- **Time-Bounded Uniqueness:** Each project is unique and non-repeatable in full.
- **Managed Uncertainty:** Projects operate in environments with risk and complexity.
- **Granular Accountability:** Execution tracked via milestones, resources, and owners.
- **Dependency on Integration:** Often dependent on contributions from multiple functions or vendors.

Fine-Tuning Parameters:
- **Persistence Score:** 3/10 — Projects are temporary by definition.
- **Flexibility Score:** 5/10 — Projects adapt within scope but have rigid guardrails.
- **Influence Radius:** 6/10 — Impacts systems, teams, and customer outcomes.
- **Sensitivity to Change Triggers:** 8/10 — Highly reactive to internal and external disruptions.
