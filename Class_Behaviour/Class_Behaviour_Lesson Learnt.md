[[Class_LessonLearnt]]

Class Name:
Lesson Learnt

Behavioral Purpose:
A **Lesson Learnt** captures the **transitional memory of an organization**—recording **insightful reflections** from past experience that guide **future improvement, risk mitigation, and strategic refinement**. It allows businesses and AI agents to **operate with hindsight awareness**, building institutional intelligence over time.

Core Behaviors:
- **Capture & Reflect** – Extracts insights from decisions, incidents, or performance outcomes.
- **Influence Change** – Informs process redesign, risk protocols, or decision rules.
- **Prevent Recurrence** – Helps teams avoid repeated errors by referencing validated patterns.
- **Transfer Wisdom** – Enables continuous learning across teams, projects, and leadership transitions.

Interaction Targets:
- **Events / Incidents / Projects** – Directly derived from real-world actions and their consequences.
- **Business Processes** – Used to enhance or refine operational steps and standard workflows.
- **Risk Management** – Feeds lessons into updated controls or preparedness frameworks.
- **Decision-Making** – Validates or challenges assumptions in strategic planning.
- **Knowledge Repositories** – Formalized into documented resources (e.g., case studies, post-mortems).

Sensing Triggers:
- **Project Closure or Sprint Retrospective** – Triggers capture of lessons.
- **Incident Response or Root Cause Analysis** – Lessons extracted post-issue.
- **Performance Deviations** – Unexpected results (positive or negative) spark inquiry.
- **Strategic Pivots or Market Shifts** – Captures why a decision was made and its outcome.

Decision Rules:
- A **Lesson Learnt** must be traceable to a concrete **Event, Process, or Decision Context**.
- `lessonImpactLevel > 0.8` implies it should be **flagged for enterprise-wide integration or practice revision**.
- Lessons tagged `lessonCategory = Strategic` should be **evaluated for future scenario planning**.
- Lessons with `lessonRetentionStatus = Deprecated` must remain accessible as historical reference.

Adaptation Behavior:
- **Generalization** – Similar lessons are clustered and abstracted into themes or root causes.
- **Escalation** – High-impact lessons may become **inputs to Standards, Policies, or Training**.
- **Lifecycle Conversion** – May transition into a **Best Practice** if validated repeatedly.
- **Feedback Loop** – Subsequent project phases validate whether the lesson remains applicable.

Lifecycle Phases:
- **Captured** – Collected from the field or project team.
- **Validated** – Confirmed with evidence and stakeholder review.
- **Integrated** – Applied into business processes, risk libraries, or knowledge artifacts.
- **Deprecated** – Deemed no longer relevant, but archived for traceability.

Special Traits:
- **Context-Rich** – Lessons are tied to **when, where, and under what conditions** they were learned.
- **Narrative-Based** – Often stored in a mix of structured fields and unstructured stories.
- **Self-Reinforcing** – Each lesson increases organizational intelligence density and lowers decision entropy.
- **Machine-Instructive** – Suitable for fine-tuning risk models, AI retrospectives, and agent performance optimization.

Fine-Tuning Parameters:
- **AI-Usability Score:** 7.8/10 – Moderately structured but needs normalization for use in RAG.
- **Knowledge Granularity:** 8.5/10 – Valuable for tactical improvement and strategic learning.
- **Causal Traceability:** 9/10 – Strong linkages to incident chains, decisions, and outcomes.
- **Reusability Rate:** 8/10 – Frequently reused in similar contexts or domains.
