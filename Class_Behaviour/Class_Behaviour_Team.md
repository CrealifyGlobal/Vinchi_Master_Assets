[[Class_Team]]

Class Name:
Team

Behavioral Purpose:
A **Team** is the social and operational unit where work is actually done. Teams embody collaboration, execution, and collective accountability. They serve as the immediate environment for delivering outcomes within broader Business Units or Work Systems and adapt dynamically to functional demands, project lifecycles, or organizational changes.

Teams translate strategy and structure into coordinated action, representing the smallest collaborative grouping with shared purpose, roles, and rhythms.

Core Behaviors:
- **Deliver Assigned Outcomes:** Execute Business Functions or projects tied to strategic or operational objectives.
- **Coordinate Roles and Responsibilities:** Share work among members based on capability, role, or specialization.
- **Interface with Systems and Structures:** Operate within policies, workflows, and Work Systems to ensure coherence and efficiency.
- **Self-Regulate and Collaborate:** Manage internal communication, norms, and peer accountability mechanisms.

Interaction Targets:
Teams interact primarily with:
- **Business Units**, which define their organizational context.
- **Business Functions**, which assign their operational mandates.
- **Work Systems**, which structure their processes and tools.
- **Projects**, when engaged in temporary initiatives.
- **People**, forming the membership and leadership of the team.

Sensing Triggers:
- Assignment to a new project or strategic initiative.
- Organizational restructuring that affects team boundaries or membership.
- Performance deviations requiring intervention or transformation.
- Collaboration needs across departments or business units.

Decision Rules:
- All Teams must belong to a Business Unit and be composed of at least one Person.
- Teams must perform or support at least one Business Function.
- Teams of type "Project-Based" must be linked to a specific Project.
- A Team must operate under the governance of an existing Work System or policy framework.

Adaptation Behavior:
- Can be dissolved, reformed, merged, or split as priorities and organizational designs evolve.
- Team composition may change due to onboarding, turnover, or reallocation.
- Performance tools, rituals, and leadership styles evolve based on team maturity and context.
- Agile or cross-functional teams adjust more rapidly to shifting business environments.

Lifecycle Phases:
- **Formed:** Members are assigned and a shared objective is defined.
- **Aligned:** Work is distributed, roles are clarified, norms are established.
- **Executing:** Tasks are performed in coordination and collaboration.
- **Evaluated:** Progress and performance are assessed through feedback and KPIs.
- **Adapted or Disbanded:** Changes in scope, performance, or relevance drive transformation or closure.

Special Traits:
- **Small-Scale but High-Impact:** Though typically small in size, teams are where execution either succeeds or fails.
- **Dynamic by Nature:** Easily reconfigurable to meet shifting business needs.
- **Social and Technical Interface:** Blend interpersonal collaboration with process, technology, and policy compliance.
- **Cultural Microcosms:** Often exhibit subcultures, rituals, and values that shape broader organizational behavior.

Fine-Tuning Parameters:
- **Persistence Score:** 6/10 — Many teams are project-based or evolving; permanence varies.
- **Flexibility Score:** 9/10 — Highly adaptable in terms of form, focus, and membership.
- **Influence Radius:** 7/10 — Strong influence within their local sphere; moderate at the enterprise level.
- **Sensitivity to Change Triggers:** 9/10 — Highly responsive to structural, strategic, and functional shifts.
