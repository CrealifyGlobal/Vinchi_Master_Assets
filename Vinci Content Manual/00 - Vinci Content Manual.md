## 📘## 📘 Vinci Content Manual – Execution Layer (Draft 1)

### 🔹 Overview: The Execution Layer

The Execution Layer of the Vinci Content System turns strategic inputs into high-impact content. It is modular, prompt-driven, and formula-guided — meaning it can be reliably followed by anyone, whether they are a strategist, writer, or assistant.

This layer is structured in **6 Phases**, each with clearly defined steps and linked artefacts.

---

## ⚙️ Phase 0: Strategic Campaign Setup

Before creating content, we always begin with a **Campaign Plan**. Every piece of content is part of a broader communication effort. If a campaign plan does not yet exist, this phase guides the user to create one.

### 🧩 Step 0.1: Campaign Plan Check

Prompt: "Do you already have a campaign plan this content belongs to?"

- If yes, select or input it.
    
- If not, the system will walk you through campaign plan creation.
    

### 🧩 Step 0.2: Create a Campaign Plan (if needed)

The system asks:

1. **Campaign Name / Title**  
    _"What’s the working title for this campaign?"_
    
2. **Theme or Pillar**  
    _"Which Vinci theme or pillar does this campaign support?"_
    
3. **Primary Goal**  
    _"What’s the goal of the campaign? (e.g., build authority, drive adoption, educate clients)"_
    
4. **Target Audience(s)**  
    _"Who is this campaign speaking to?"_
    
5. **Core Message / Strategic Angle**  
    _"What belief or idea are you trying to communicate?"_
    
6. **Timeframe & Cadence**  
    _"What’s the schedule? (e.g., 3 weeks, 2 pieces per week)"_
    
7. **Channels / Output Types**  
    _"What formats and platforms will this campaign use?"_
    

The result is a **Campaign Brief Artefact** that stays attached to all future content in the campaign.

---

## ⚙️ Phase 1: Define Intent & Inputs

### 🧩 Step 1.1: Select Content Channel (Output Type)

Prompt: "What type of content are you creating?"

- Examples: Blog article, LinkedIn post, Microlearning script, Training video, Case study, Email newsletter, Video ad
    
- _This selection determines structural requirements and required inputs._
    

### 🧩 Step 1.2: Define the Audience

Prompt: "Who is this content for?"

- Specify persona, maturity level, industry, familiarity with topic
    

### 🧩 Step 1.3: Clarify the Content Goal

Prompt: "What do you want this content to do?"

- Examples: Educate, convert, inspire, build authority, engage internally
    

### 🧩 Step 1.4: Provide Inputs / Source Materials

Prompt: "What notes or materials do you already have?"

- Examples: Reports, research docs, transcripts, insights, audio notes
    

### 🧩 Step 1.5: Identify Any Channel-Specific Dependencies

Prompt: "Does this output require special inputs?"

- Example: Curriculum for training script, campaign theme for carousel, visual references for video
    

---

## ⚙️ Phase 2: Formula Selection

### 🧩 Step 2.1: Recommended Formula(s)

The system suggests 1–3 best-fit formulas based on your intent, channel, and audience.

- Examples: PAS, AIDA, Use Case Article, Playbook Template, Trend Deep-Dive
    

### 🧩 Step 2.2: Confirm or Combine Formulas

Prompt: "Would you like to use one of these, or combine formulas?"

- Optional: Mix (e.g., AIDA + FAB, Use Case + Trend Deep-Dive)
    

### 🧩 Step 2.3: Preview Formula Structures

Each formula includes:

- Name, Purpose, Writing Style, Key Characteristics
    
- Structure/Sections
    
- Ideal Use Cases, Audience Fit, and Examples
    

---

## ⚙️ Phase 3: Outline Builder

### 🧩 Step 3.1: Auto-Generate a Formula-Based Outline

Prompt: "Here’s a suggested outline based on your formula(s). Would you like to modify it?"

- Example (Use Case Article):
    
    - Title & Subtitle
        
    - Introduction
        
    - Challenge
        
    - Implementation
        
    - Results & Impact
        
    - Conclusion & CTA
        

### 🧩 Step 3.2: Customize the Outline

Prompt: "Would you like to add any specific points, data, or personal stories to each section?"

---

## ⚙️ Phase 4: Draft Composer

### 🧩 Step 4.1: Section-by-Section Guided Drafting

Each outline section triggers a custom writing prompt.

- Example (Introduction): "Write 100–150 words about the context and why this matters. Start with a personal insight or bold statement."
    

### 🧩 Step 4.2: Tone & Style Selector

Prompt: "Would you like the tone to be professional, conversational, witty, or instructional?"

- Style adjusts based on formula or platform norms.
    

---

## ⚙️ Phase 5: Polish & Package

### 🧩 Step 5.1: Write the Headline & Subtitle

Use hook formulas like 4 U’s, PPPP, or 3 Reasons Why

### 🧩 Step 5.2: Add CTA & Social Wrapper

Prompt: "Let’s write a call to action that fits your goal. Want to generate a social post preview?"

### 🧩 Step 5.3: Review & Finalize

Checklist:

- Clear structure
    
- Proofed tone
    
- CTA and title included
    
- Ready for publishing or repurposing

### 🟨 Persuasion & Conversion

```
Name: AIDA
Purpose: Move audience toward action
Style: Structured, persuasive
Traits: Psychological progression, CTA focus
Structure: Attention → Interest → Desire → Action
Use Cases: Ads, landing pages, sales copy
Fit: B2B/B2C leads in decision mode
```

```
Name: PAS
Purpose: Trigger urgency and resolution
Style: Empathetic, sharp
Traits: Problem-Agitate-Solution flow
Structure: Problem → Agitate → Solution
Use Cases: Emails, webinars, Facebook ads
Fit: Leads with pain points or unmet needs
```

```
Name: BAB
Purpose: Show transformation
Style: Aspirational, contrast-based
Traits: Before–After–Bridge framing
Structure: Before → After → Bridge
Use Cases: Intro copy, sales pages
Fit: Growth seekers or transformation buyers
```

```
Name: FAB
Purpose: Translate product features into value
Style: Technical → Benefit-driven
Traits: Feature–Advantage–Benefit hierarchy
Structure: Feature → Advantage → Benefit
Use Cases: Product pages, sales decks, specs
Fit: Rational buyers, engineers, decision-makers
```

```
Name: 3 Reasons Why
Purpose: Convince through direct, credible logic
Style: Assertive, structured
Traits: Lists reasons with proof and urgency
Structure: Why we’re best → Why you should believe us → Why to act now
Use Cases: B2B offers, proposals, landing pages
Fit: Warm leads, B2B buyers
```

```
Name: 4 U’s
Purpose: Write high-performing hooks
Style: Concise, scannable
Traits: Urgent, Useful, Unique, Ultra-Specific
Structure: Use as a checklist in intros/headlines
Use Cases: Email subjects, carousel openers
Fit: Skimming audiences, social readers
```

```
Name: 4 C’s
Purpose: Write strong, trustworthy messaging
Style: Clear, credible, consistent, competitive
Traits: Messaging integrity and sharpness
Structure: Guiding principles, not steps
Use Cases: Brand messaging, B2B web copy, internal docs
Fit: Professional buyers, long-term trust building
```

```
Name: 5 Objections
Purpose: Overcome common conversion blockers
Style: Empathetic, strategic
Traits: Address Time, Money, Trust, Need, Effectiveness
Structure: One section per objection
Use Cases: Sales pages, objection-handling emails
Fit: Late-stage prospects
```

## 🧭 Strategic Layer: Themes, Movements & Campaign Logic

### 🔹 Themes (Vinci Pillars)

Themes are Vinci’s long-term narrative focus areas. Each piece of content and campaign supports one or more themes.

Each Theme Includes:

- Theme Title
    
- Theme Statement (What Vinci believes and why it matters)
    
- Supporting Messages (Core talking points)
    
- Associated Campaigns
    
- Relevant Audience Segments
    

> _Example:_  
> **Theme:** "Process as Creative Leverage"  
> **Belief:** Vinci believes process isn’t a constraint — it’s an amplifier for creative work.

### 🔹 Market Movements

Themes should reflect or challenge market signals. Trackable signals include:

- Emerging trends
    
- Shifts in audience behavior
    
- Competitive messaging gaps
    
- Regulatory or tech changes
    

Each movement can be tied to one or more themes and campaigns.

---

### 🔹 Campaign Hierarchy & Matrix

Campaigns sit under themes and align with real-world events, product launches, seasons, or strategic pushes.

|Campaign|Theme|Goal|Audience|Status|Duration|
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
|Vinci OS Launch|Process as Leverage|Build Product Awareness|Early Adopters|In Production|Q2|
|Creators & AI|Future of Creative Work|Thought Leadership|Creators, Designers|In Planning|6 Weeks|

This matrix lives in a central planning dashboard and is referenced during every Phase 0 setup.

## 🧠 Persona System & Tagging Logic

### 🔹 Persona Profiles

Each persona defines a distinct audience segment. Content creators select or define personas early in the process (Phase 1).

Each Persona Includes:

- Persona Name / Label (e.g. Growth Marketer, HR Leader, Junior Designer)
    
- Goals & Pain Points
    
- Preferred Tone (e.g. inspirational, tactical, insightful)
    
- Knowledge Level / Maturity
    
- Platforms They Use Most
    
- Content Types They Prefer (e.g. visuals, deep dives, summaries)
    

### 🔹 Tagging Framework

Content is tagged with the following dimensions:

- **Theme** (linked to Vinci’s narrative structure)
    
- **Campaign** (linked to planning matrix)
    
- **Persona(s)** (one or multiple)
    
- **Use Case** (internal training, sales enablement, public thought leadership, etc.)
    
- **Channel / Format**
    
- **Content Status** (draft, review, approved, published)
    

These tags are used in:

- The dashboard
    
- Naming conventions
    
- File organization
    
- Brief generation and filtering
    

### 🔹 Use Cases as Creative Filters

Instead of just audience → message, Vinci also includes **Use Case** as a filter:

- Product Launches
    
- Training & L&D
    
- Internal Culture
    
- Recruitment Campaigns
    
- Thought Leadership
    
- Sales Enablement
    

Each use case has recommended formats, channels, and formulas. This allows campaigns to scale while staying relevant to context.

Each playbook is a ready-made guide for producing content within a specific business scenario.

Each Playbook Includes:

- Summary of the Use Case
    
- Common Goals & Content Types
    
- Recommended Formulas
    
- Template Links
    
- Suggested Channels & Distribution Notes
    
- Do's and Don'ts
    
- Metrics to Watch
    

> _Example: Playbook for L&D:_
> 
> - Use: Internal knowledge-sharing, skill training
>     
> - Formats: Microlearning scripts, training slides, recap videos
>     
> - Formulas: Microlearning Script, L&D Article
>     
> - Channels: LMS, internal newsletter, Slack
>     

### 🔹 Brief Generator Logic

The system generates a brief before content creation begins.

Each Brief Includes:

- Content Type
    
- Campaign & Theme
    
- Goal & Outcome
    
- Audience Profile(s)
    
- Use Case & Context
    
- Reference Materials / Inputs
    
- Suggested Formula(s)
    
- Tone / Style Preferences
    

> These briefs ensure alignment and are saved for QA, repurposing, and audits.
