[[Class_Strategy_Map]]

Class Name:
Strategy Map

Behavioral Purpose:
A Strategy Map provides a visual framework that captures how an organization’s strategic objectives connect to value creation across multiple perspectives. It clarifies the cause-and-effect relationships between goals, initiatives, enablers, and KPIs, ensuring that all parts of the organization understand how their work contributes to broader strategic success. Strategy Maps serve as essential communication tools for leadership alignment, resource prioritization, and performance management, bridging the gap between abstract strategy and practical execution.

Core Behaviors:
- **Visualize Strategic Relationships:** Strategy Maps graphically depict how different objectives, initiatives, and performance measures link together to achieve the organization's strategy.
- **Align Business Perspectives:** They structure goals and enablers across key dimensions such as Financial, Customer, Internal Processes, and Learning & Growth, ensuring balanced execution.
- **Support Decision-Making:** Strategy Maps provide a reference framework for prioritizing actions, allocating resources, and navigating trade-offs between competing initiatives.
- **Drive Execution Monitoring:** Through mapped KPIs and performance indicators, Strategy Maps make strategic progress measurable and visible.

Interaction Targets:
Strategy Maps primarily interact with:
- **Strategy**, serving as the visual representation of how strategic goals will be realized.
- **Objectives**, linking specific outcomes across business perspectives.
- **Business Perspectives**, ensuring holistic coverage of critical organizational dimensions.
- **Decisions**, informing executive and operational decision-making frameworks.
- **Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)**, providing performance tracking mechanisms for mapped objectives.

Sensing Triggers:
- Changes in strategic priorities requiring adjustment of mapped relationships or focus areas.
- Emergence of new capabilities or market conditions that require realignment of strategy execution pathways.
- Leadership reviews or strategic planning cycles prompting refinement of the map.
- Performance data from KPIs indicating that assumed cause-and-effect relationships need reassessment.

Decision Rules:
- If mapped objectives and KPIs continue to reflect strategic execution effectively, maintain the current Strategy Map structure.
- If misalignments or bottlenecks emerge, update the Strategy Map to reflect new realities, ensuring coherence and balance across perspectives.
- Ensure that any adjustments to the Strategy Map are validated through cross-functional leadership engagement.
- Keep the map dynamic but maintain its core integrity to avoid strategic drift.

Adaptation Behavior:
- Introduce new strategic objectives, KPIs, or enablers into the map as business needs evolve.
- Remove or modify links between objectives if causal assumptions no longer hold true.
- Shift emphasis between perspectives (e.g., from operational efficiency to customer intimacy) when strategic focus changes.
- Refresh the visualization structure if the organization's business model or execution approach fundamentally transforms.

Lifecycle Phases:
- **Designed:** Strategy Map is created based on the organization’s strategic plan and objectives.
- **Adopted:** The Strategy Map is formally communicated and integrated into planning and execution frameworks.
- **Activated:** Organizational initiatives, KPIs, and resource allocations are aligned with the map.
- **Monitored and Adjusted:** Ongoing performance monitoring prompts refinements to ensure the map remains a true reflection of execution reality.
- **Evolved or Replaced:** As strategies evolve significantly, the Strategy Map is updated or rebuilt to reflect the new strategic landscape.

Special Traits:
- **High Alignment Power:** Strategy Maps create strategic coherence across siloed functions and initiatives.
- **Dynamic Flexibility:** They are designed to adapt as execution feedback and environmental changes occur.
- **Strong Visualization Focus:** Strategy Maps excel in making complex strategic interdependencies clear and actionable.

Fine-Tuning Parameters:
- **Persistence Score:** 7/10 — Designed to endure across multiple planning cycles but flexible for periodic refinement.
- **Flexibility Score:** 8/10 — Capable of evolving quickly when strategic shifts occur.
- **Influence Radius:** 9/10 — Impacts executive decision-making, operational planning, and performance measurement organization-wide.
- **Sensitivity to Change Triggers:** 7/10 — Responsive to strategic performance feedback, organizational learning, and environmental dynamics.
