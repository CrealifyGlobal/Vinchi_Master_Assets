[[Class_Program]]

Class Name:
Program

Behavioral Purpose:
A **Program** serves as a **strategic coordination mechanism** that unifies related projects under a common governance structure to **realize long-term benefits**, manage interdependencies, and align initiatives with enterprise-wide goals. It acts as the **bridge between project execution and portfolio governance**, optimizing performance, resources, and strategic impact across grouped initiatives.

Core Behaviors:
- **Thematic Coordination:** Groups projects that share a common objective, domain, or benefit theme.
- **Oversight & Governance:** Enforces standards, methodologies, and risk control across its projects.
- **Strategic Delivery Enablement:** Translates strategic objectives into executable workstreams.
- **Benefit Realization:** Tracks cumulative value delivery beyond individual project outputs.
- **Dependency Management:** Identifies, resolves, and communicates cross-project impacts and risks.

Interaction Targets:
- **Project Portfolio**, which provides overarching prioritization and resource governance.
- **Projects**, which are the operational units delivering outcomes under the program.
- **Strategic Objectives**, which define the outcomes the program is expected to fulfill.
- **Policies**, particularly those establishing governance, compliance, and management frameworks.
- **Metrics**, which evaluate program health, delivery progress, and realized value.

Sensing Triggers:
- Formation of multiple related projects or initiatives.
- Strategic decision to consolidate execution under a single leadership model.
- Emerging cross-project risks or resource conflicts requiring coordinated resolution.
- Change in enterprise priorities requiring collective response.
- Inadequate progress or alignment of isolated projects to strategic goals.

Decision Rules:
- Every Program must belong to at least one Project Portfolio.
- A Program must include at least one Project under its governance.
- If a Program is compliance-related, it must reference a Regulatory Policy.
- Programs must be evaluated using Program Metrics (e.g., benefit realization rate, resource utilization).
- A Program aligned to a Strategic Objective must justify its project groupings based on thematic coherence.

Adaptation Behavior:
- **Project Reassignment:** Projects may enter or exit the program based on evolving alignment or performance.
- **Scope Refinement:** Adjusts thematic focus and boundaries based on strategy shifts or external changes.
- **Budget Realignment:** Resources are reallocated across projects based on priority and progress.
- **Governance Updates:** Adopts new policies or KPIs as the organization’s maturity or objectives evolve.

Lifecycle Phases:
- **Initiated:** Program theme, scope, objectives, and governance framework are defined.
- **Formed:** Projects are selected, initiated, and resourced under the program.
- **Executed:** Projects are managed with coordinated oversight and shared practices.
- **Monitored:** Program-wide KPIs and risks are tracked to guide decision-making.
- **Closed or Evolved:** Program is retired upon completion or transformed to align with new strategic themes.

Special Traits:
- **Intermediate Aggregation Layer:** Sits between operational execution (Projects) and strategic control (Portfolios).
- **Cross-Cutting Scope:** Can span multiple departments, domains, or geographies.
- **Theme-Centric:** Anchored by a business outcome or capability area rather than a singular deliverable.
- **Long-Term Focus:** Programs often last longer than individual projects and prioritize sustained benefit realization.

Fine-Tuning Parameters:
- **Persistence Score:** 7/10 — Programs typically outlast projects but are not indefinite.
- **Flexibility Score:** 6/10 — Internal composition (projects) changes more frequently than governance.
- **Influence Radius:** 8/10 — Direct impact on project governance, indirect on strategy.
- **Sensitivity to Change Triggers:** 7/10 — Programs are moderately sensitive to enterprise-level shifts.
