[[Class_Business_Processes]]

Class Name:
Business Processes

Behavioral Purpose:
Business Processes define the structured, repeatable flows of work that guide how value is created, supported, or governed within an organization. They are essential to operationalizing strategy, delivering consistent outcomes, and enabling scalability. Business Processes sit at the intersection of people, technology, and governance, providing a standardized way to execute core, support, and management functions. Unlike Capabilities, which define what an organization can do, Business Processes define how it gets done.

Core Behaviors:
- **Enable Operational Execution:** Processes turn strategic objectives and capabilities into actionable workflows.
- **Ensure Consistency and Compliance:** Defined procedures reduce variance, improve quality, and support regulatory alignment.
- **Drive Efficiency and Improvement:** Processes are the primary focus of optimization, automation, and digital transformation initiatives.
- **Coordinate Functional Collaboration:** Business Processes orchestrate tasks across departments and systems, ensuring cohesive execution.

Interaction Targets:
Business Processes primarily interact with:
- **Business Functions**, which are responsible for the design, execution, and oversight of specific processes.
- **Capabilities**, serving as vehicles to operationalize and reinforce organizational strengths.
- **Events**, such as triggers, exceptions, or milestones that initiate or alter the flow of a process.
- **Policies**, providing rules and constraints under which processes must operate.
- **Technologies**, such as workflow automation tools, ERP systems, or AI engines, supporting execution.

Sensing Triggers:
- Changes in regulation or compliance requirements necessitating process redesign or additional controls.
- Performance indicators (e.g., cost, cycle time, error rate) signaling inefficiencies or bottlenecks.
- Strategic shifts (e.g., digital transformation, expansion, new customer journey expectations) demanding new or modified processes.
- Technological advancements allowing for increased automation or smarter decisioning.

Decision Rules:
- Processes should be mapped and documented clearly, with defined inputs, outputs, and responsibilities.
- Assign a process owner for each key process to ensure accountability, optimization, and compliance.
- Prioritize processes for improvement based on impact on customer experience, regulatory exposure, or cost.
- Implement monitoring and analytics to track execution quality, identify issues, and drive continuous improvement.

Adaptation Behavior:
- Refactor processes in response to operational issues, feedback, or emerging best practices.
- Introduce new processes to accommodate shifts in business models, customer needs, or strategic direction.
- Automate repeatable, rules-based process segments to increase speed and reduce errors.
- Replace legacy manual processes with digital workflows when operational maturity or capability allows.

Lifecycle Phases:
- **Defined:** The process is scoped, mapped, and assigned to a responsible function or team.
- **Operationalized:** The process is implemented and actively used in daily operations.
- **Monitored:** Key performance indicators are tracked to assess execution quality and identify optimization opportunities.
- **Optimized:** The process is improved through automation, lean redesign, or policy refinement.
- **Retired or Replaced:** Obsolete processes are phased out or merged as business needs evolve.

Special Traits:
- **Scalable and Repeatable:** Processes are designed to support consistency across scale and complexity.
- **Subject to Continuous Improvement:** Business Processes are never static—they evolve through feedback, innovation, and maturity.
- **Multifunctional in Nature:** Most processes span across multiple teams, systems, and decision-making roles.

Fine-Tuning Parameters:
- **Persistence Score:** 8/10 — Once established, processes tend to persist, but they must adapt to stay relevant and compliant.
- **Flexibility Score:** 7/10 — Processes can be refined and optimized but require structure and discipline for stability.
- **Influence Radius:** 9/10 — Business Processes are central to execution, quality, customer experience, and operational agility.
- **Sensitivity to Change Triggers:** 8/10 — Highly responsive to strategic change, technology adoption, performance gaps, and compliance shifts.
